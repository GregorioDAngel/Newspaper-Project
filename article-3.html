By: Gregorio Del Angel

Young Hotel Guard Dies Preventing Catastrophic Attack

Baghdad — Twenty-one-year-old hotel guard Hasib Mohamed Jaafar is being remembered as a hero after sacrificing his life to stop a garbage truck bomb from entering the Sadeer Novotel Hotel in central Baghdad.

According to witnesses, the truck accelerated toward the hotel entrance shortly before sunset. Realizing the danger, Hasib reportedly opened fire on the driver moments before the explosives detonated. Security officials believe his actions forced the bomber to trigger the explosion outside the gates rather than inside the crowded building.

The blast killed Hasib instantly and sent smoke, glass, and debris flying across nearby streets. Several civilians were injured, and surrounding businesses sustained major damage. Witnesses described scenes of confusion as survivors stumbled through dust-filled streets searching for loved ones.

At his family home in Sadr City, mourners gathered to honor the young guard, whose remains were barely recoverable after the explosion. Relatives buried what little could be found, while his wife and mother openly wept beside the coffin.

Neighbors described Hasib as quiet, hardworking, and deeply committed to supporting his family despite the dangers of working security in Baghdad’s unstable environment.

The bombing has renewed concerns over growing attacks targeting hotels, government sites, and civilian infrastructure throughout the capital. Security experts warn that insurgent groups increasingly rely on large vehicle bombs capable of causing mass casualties even when attacks fail to reach their intended targets.

For many Iraqis, Hasib’s death symbolizes the impossible choices ordinary citizens face daily—risking their lives simply by going to work.
