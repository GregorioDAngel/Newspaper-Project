Massive Explosion Devastates Tayaran Square, Civilians Among Dead

Baghdad — A powerful car bomb tore through Tayaran Square early Sunday morning, leaving behind burned vehicles, shattered storefronts, and dozens of civilian casualties in one of the deadliest attacks the city has seen in recent weeks. The explosion occurred during the busy morning rush as laborers gathered in the square searching for temporary work and buses filled with commuters passed through the area.

Witnesses described a sudden fireball followed by thick black smoke that could be seen across central Baghdad. Nearby cars collided in panic as people fled the scene, while injured victims screamed for help amid burning debris and twisted metal. Emergency responders and civilians rushed to carry survivors away before secondary fires spread.

“I heard the blast and then all the windows shook,” said a shopkeeper whose business overlooks the square. “When the smoke cleared, bodies were lying everywhere.”

The attack caused widespread destruction throughout nearby neighborhoods, including Bataween, where residents reported cracked walls, collapsed ceilings, and broken windows. Many families spent the rest of the day cleaning debris from their homes while fearing another attack could happen at any moment.

Hospitals struggled to manage the number of injured arriving within minutes of the bombing. Doctors at Kindi Hospital reported severe burns, shrapnel wounds, and psychological trauma among survivors. Relatives gathered outside emergency rooms searching desperately for missing family members.

Government officials condemned the attack and blamed insurgent groups attempting to destabilize the city. However, many Baghdad residents expressed frustration, saying bombings have become so common that promises of improved security no longer inspire confidence.

For many civilians, the explosion was not simply another headline but another reminder that ordinary life in Baghdad can disappear in seconds.
