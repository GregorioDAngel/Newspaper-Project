Neighborhood Rumors Grow After Junk Dealer Collects Human Remains

Baghdad — Residents of the Bataween district have become increasingly disturbed by reports surrounding local junk dealer Hadi al-Attag, who allegedly spent days collecting human remains from bomb sites across the city.

According to neighbors and customers from nearby coffee shops, Hadi claimed he was attempting to create a “complete corpse” from scattered body parts left behind after explosions. He reportedly argued that many victims of bombings were buried incomplete or unidentified, denying them dignity in death.

“Hadi always tells stories,” said Aziz, owner of a local café frequented by residents and journalists. “Sometimes people laugh at him, but lately the things he says are harder to ignore.”

Witnesses claim Hadi stored the remains inside an abandoned structure known locally as the “Jewish ruin,” a deteriorating building long associated with ghost stories and urban legends. The structure has become the subject of intense speculation, with some residents refusing to walk near it after dark.

While many dismiss the rumors as the drunken fantasies of an eccentric man, others believe the story reflects the brutal reality of Baghdad’s violence. In recent months, bombings have left countless bodies dismembered or impossible to identify, forcing families to bury partial remains or empty coffins.

Human rights advocates say the situation highlights the emotional toll war continues to inflict on civilians. “When society reaches a point where people feel compelled to gather body parts from the streets, that reveals a complete collapse of dignity and security,” one local observer stated.

Authorities have not launched an official investigation into the claims, but the story continues spreading rapidly throughout Baghdad’s neighborhoods and coffee shops.
